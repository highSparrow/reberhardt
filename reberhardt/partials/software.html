Places I've Worked

  • 2015

    Sandia National Laboratories: Computational Science Research Intern

    I interned for Sandia National Labs' Scalable Algorithms Team developing an optimized block sparse matrix-vector multiplication (BSpMV) algorithm for manycore architectures (including Sandy Bridge, Knights Corner, and Kepler). The algorithm I developed outperforms the comparable algorithm in Intel's Math Kernel Library (MKL) by up to 3x and outperforms the equivalent algorithm in Nvidia's cuSPARSE by up to 4x.

  • 2015

    Recon Sports: Software Consultant

    Recon Sports pairs younger hockey players with professional advisors and helps them get recruited into college hockey leagues. I built an initial version of Recon's software platform. It allows players to enter game information, schedules "scouts" to visit players' games and score their playing in person, allows recruiters to search for players using specific criteria, and more.

  • 2013-2015

    Navier Inc: Cofounder and Lead Backend Developer

    At Navier, I built a platform (Anvil) that enables developers to interact directly with customers in the concept and prototyping stages of feature development (watch the pitch). This beats the alternative of making a release, gathering feedback, realizing customers don't understand how a feature is supposed to work, making another release, collecting feedback again, and so on (a long, iterative cycle). I also consulted for several companies, including the Chicago Sun Times, Jared Polis for Congress, and JARST LLC.
